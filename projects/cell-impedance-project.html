Project Overview

The Cell Impedance Project investigates the electrical impedance characteristics of biological cells for medical diagnostics and research applications. This interdisciplinary research combines electrical engineering principles with biomedical sciences to develop novel diagnostic tools and understand cellular behavior under various conditions.

Research Objectives

  • Characterize electrical impedance properties of different cell types
  • Develop measurement techniques for accurate impedance analysis
  • Investigate the relationship between cell health and impedance characteristics
  • Design and optimize impedance measurement systems
  • Explore applications in medical diagnostics and drug testing

Technical Approach

Impedance Spectroscopy

Utilizing electrical impedance spectroscopy to measure the complex impedance of biological cells across a range of frequencies, providing insights into cellular structure and function.

Microelectrode Design

Design and fabrication of microelectrode arrays for precise electrical measurements of individual cells and cell populations.

Signal Processing

Advanced signal processing techniques to extract meaningful information from impedance measurements and distinguish between different cell states.

Technologies & Tools

Electronics Biomedical Engineering Signal Processing Impedance Spectroscopy Microelectrode Arrays Data Analysis LabVIEW

Key Contributions

  • Designed and implemented impedance measurement circuits
  • Developed data acquisition systems for real-time impedance monitoring
  • Built back and frontend of a python program for control of syringe pump, microscope image, real-time lightweight computer vision model to identify and classify different cell types, cell velocities, cell position prediction, and cell control on a micron scale
  • Conducted experiments with various cell types and conditions
  • Analyzed impedance data to identify characteristic patterns
  • Collaborated with biomedical researchers on experimental protocols
  • Contributed to system optimization and calibration procedures

Applications

Medical Diagnostics

Potential applications in non-invasive cell health monitoring and early disease detection through impedance-based biomarkers.

Drug Testing

Real-time monitoring of cellular responses to pharmaceutical compounds for drug development and toxicity testing.

Research Tools

Advanced instrumentation for fundamental research in cell biology and biophysics.

Results & Impact

This research contributes to the development of novel diagnostic tools that could revolutionize medical testing by providing:

  • Non-invasive cell health assessment
  • Real-time monitoring capabilities
  • High-throughput screening potential
  • Cost-effective diagnostic solutions

Future Work

The project continues to explore advanced measurement techniques and expand applications. Future research directions include:

  • Integration with microfluidic systems
  • Machine learning for automated cell classification
  • Clinical validation studies
  • Development of portable diagnostic devices
